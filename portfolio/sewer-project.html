JW11017 Lenasia Southeast Basin Sewer Upgrade

Client: Johannesburg Water (SOC) Ltd

Location: Lenasia, Gauteng

Project Value: R7 Million

Duration: 8 months (2023)

Project Overview

This critical infrastructure project involved the replacement of aging asbestos cement (AC) sewer pipelines with modern uPVC piping systems in the Lenasia Southeast Basin area. The upgrade addressed chronic blockages that were causing environmental and health hazards for the community.

Scope of Work

  • 200-315mm sewer pipeline replacement
  • 1.2km of new uPVC piping
  • 48 new house connections
  • 5 new inspection chambers

Technical Specifications

  • Material: uPVC Class 34
  • Trench depth: 1.8-2.4m
  • Bedding: 19mm stone with geotextile
  • Backfill: Selected imported material

Construction Methodology

The project utilized open trench construction methods with careful traffic management plans. Special attention was given to:

  • Precise laser-guided grading for optimal flow
  • Mechanical jointing of uPVC pipes
  • Compaction testing at every 150mm layer
  • Daily CS10 pipe alignment checks

Typical pipe bedding detail (Not to scale)

Key Challenge & Solution

Challenge: High groundwater table in sections causing trench instability

Solution: Implemented well-point dewatering system and used geotextile-wrapped stone bedding to prevent pipe flotation and maintain stability during backfilling.

Project Outcome

  • Improved flow capacity by 40%
