10th Street Rennie Road

Gravel Road Rehabilitation & Solar Lighting

← Back to Projects

Project Overview

This municipal infrastructure project involved the complete rehabilitation of a 1.2km gravel road section, including drainage improvements and installation of solar-powered street lighting.

Project Details

Client: City of Johannesburg

Location: Brakpan, Gauteng

Value: R1.4 Million

Duration: 3 months (2023)

Technical Specifications

Road Length: 1,200 meters

Surface: Gravel with laterite base

Drainage: V-drains and culverts

Lighting: 15 solar street lights

Scope of Work

  • Full-depth reclamation of existing roadbed
  • Installation of 600mm diameter culverts
  • Construction of 1.5km V-drain system
  • Placement of 150mm laterite base course
  • Application of 75mm gravel wearing course
  • Installation of solar street lighting
  • Road signage and markings

Key Achievements

Performance

Results

Project Challenges

Live Traffic Management

Maintaining access for residents during construction required careful phasing. We implemented a rotating one-lane system with flagmen and temporary signage to ensure continuous access.

Clay Soil Conditions

The high clay content required soil stabilization. We incorporated lime treatment into the base course and increased drainage capacity to prevent future softening.
