JW11017 Lenasia Southeast Basin

200-315mm Sewer Pipeline Upgrade

← Back to Projects

Project Overview

This critical sewer infrastructure project involved the replacement of aging asbestos cement pipelines with modern uPVC systems in Lenasia's Southeast Basin area, addressing chronic blockages and overflows affecting the community.

Project Details

Client: Johannesburg Water (SOC) Ltd

Location: Lenasia, Gauteng

Value: R7 Million

Duration: 5 months (2023)

Technical Specifications

Pipe Material: uPVC Class 34

Diameter: 200-315mm

Length: 2.3km total

Method: Open trench excavation

Scope of Work

  • Removal of existing AC sewer pipes
  • Installation of new uPVC pipelines
  • Construction of 28 new inspection chambers
  • 65 household connection upgrades
  • Bedding with 19mm stone and geotextile
  • Full road surface reinstatement
  • CS10 alignment testing

Key Achievements

Performance

✔ Zero safety incidents

✔ Completed within budget

✔ 98% of residents satisfied in survey

Results

✔ Eliminated 100% of chronic blockages

✔ Improved flow capacity by 45%

✔ Reduced maintenance calls by 80%

Project Challenges

High Groundwater

The water-saturated soil required continuous dewatering during excavation. We implemented well-point dewatering systems and used geotextile-wrapped stone bedding to prevent pipe flotation.

Traffic Management

Working in a dense residential area required careful traffic planning. We implemented phased construction zones and temporary pedestrian walkways to maintain access throughout the project.
