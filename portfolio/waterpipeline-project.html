JW 12029 Lombardy West

110mm Water Pipeline Replacement

← Back to Projects

Project Overview

This critical infrastructure project involved the replacement of aging asbestos cement (AC) water pipes with modern high-impact uPVC piping systems in the Lombardy West area. The upgrade addressed frequent pipe bursts and water supply disruptions affecting the community.

Project Details

Client: Johannesburg Water (SOC) Ltd

Location: Lombardy West, Gauteng

Value: R1.8 Million

Duration: 3 months (2023)

Technical Specifications

Pipe Material: uPVC Class 12

Diameter: 110mm

Length: 850 meters

Method: Horizontal drilling

Project Scope

  • Replacement of existing asbestos cement water mains
  • Installation of new uPVC pipes using trenchless technology
  • Connection of 35 household service lines
  • Installation of 2 new pressure control valves
  • Replacement of 4 fire hydrants
  • Road surface reinstatement

Key Achievements

Performance

✔ Zero safety incidents

✔ Completed 2 weeks ahead of schedule

✔ 100% of households reconnected within 24hrs

Results

✔ Eliminated pipe bursts in the area

✔ Reduced water losses by 65%

✔ Improved water pressure consistency

Project Challenges

High Water Table

The area's high water table required specialized dewatering equipment during excavation. We implemented well-point dewatering systems to maintain dry working conditions.

Traffic Management

Working in a busy residential area required careful traffic planning. We implemented phased work schedules and temporary pedestrian pathways to minimize disruptions.
