JW 13804 Zamimpilo

Informal Settlement Ablution Facilities

← Back to Projects

Project Overview

This community sanitation project involved the construction of modern ablution facilities in the Zamimpilo informal settlement, providing dignified sanitation solutions to over 200 households previously reliant on communal portable toilets.

Project Details

Client: Johannesburg Water (SOC) Ltd

Location: Zamimpilo Informal Settlement, Gauteng

Value: R18.2 Million

Duration: 6 months (2023)

Facility Specifications

Units Constructed: 12 Ablution Blocks

Features per Unit: 2 Toilets, 2 Showers, 2 Washbasins

Pipework: 160mm uPVC sewer connections

Water Supply: Metered standpipes

Key Features

  • Durable precast concrete structures
  • 24/7 security lighting
  • Disability-friendly access
  • Water-saving flush systems
  • Gray water drainage systems
  • Community maintenance training program

Community Impact

Health Benefits

✔ 75% reduction in waterborne diseases

✔ Eliminated open defecation in the area

✔ Improved hygiene practices

Social Benefits

✔ Increased safety for women and children

✔ Reduced queue times for facilities

✔ Created 15 local maintenance jobs

Project Challenges

High-Density Settlement

The tightly-packed informal structures required precise planning for facility placement. We conducted community consultations to identify optimal locations that served maximum residents while maintaining accessibility.

Ground Conditions

The unstable soil required specialized foundation solutions. We implemented geotechnical surveys and used reinforced concrete rafts to ensure structural stability on the uneven terrain.
